Galactic Encryption Challenge

In the distant future, the Intergalactic Cryptography Society hosts an annual event called the "Galactic Encryption Challenge." Participants from across the cosmos compete to solve complex encryption puzzles using their coding skills. This year's challenge involves decoding a message using a combination of birth month-based encryption and advanced algorithms.

As a young coder, you've stumbled upon an encrypted message. To decrypt it, you need to follow a series of steps based on your birth month:

  1. If your birth month is a prime number, you should perform a Caesar cipher decryption with a shift equal to your birth month.
  2. If your birth month is even, you should use a Vigen√®re cipher decryption with a keyword derived from the Fibonacci sequence with a length equal to your birth month.
  3. If your birth month is odd and not prime, you should decrypt using a Rail Fence cipher with a number of rails equal to your birth month.
  4. If your birth month is a perfect square, you should use a Playfair cipher decryption with a keyword derived from the square root of your birth month.

The encrypted message you've found is: "Lxsvi lezi, wli qsvi gsppv mx gvveki er."

Given your birth month, what is the decrypted message?

A) "Hello there, the force shall guide us."
B) "Hello there, the code is strong in this one."
C) "Hello there, the dark side awaits you."
D) "Hello there, the adventure begins now."
