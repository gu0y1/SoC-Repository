The Birthday Solar Panel Project

Alex, a high school student passionate about renewable energy, decides to design a solar-powered charging station for smartphones as a science project. Inspired by the global need for sustainable energy sources, Alex aims to calculate the optimal angle at which to set the solar panels for maximum efficiency. The angle depends on the month of the year to ensure the panels get the most sunlight. Alex knows that the optimal tilt angle of the panel from the horizontal should approximately equal the latitude of the location adjusted by a factor that changes with the seasons.

Alex lives in a town located at 45 degrees latitude. The adjustment factor varies as follows:

  • In winter (December, January, February), add 15 degrees.
  • In spring (March, April, May), subtract 2.5 degrees.
  • In summer (June, July, August), subtract 5 degrees.
  • In fall (September, October, November), add 5 degrees.

Question:

Given that Alex's birth month is the input parameter (from 1 to 12), what would be the optimal tilt angle for Alex's solar panels during their birth month? Assume Alex wants to adjust the panels specifically for the month they were born in. Options:
  1. 37.5 degrees
  2. 40 degrees
  3. 50 degrees
  4. 60 degrees

Instructions to Solve:

To solve this question, identify Alex's birth month and determine the season it falls in. Then, adjust the base latitude of 45 degrees according to the rules provided for the season to find the optimal tilt angle.
